# Physical GTM Ideas -- LV2 Park

Parked April 17, 2026.

Two ideas for offline/physical distribution in the Lakeview / Wrigleyville neighborhood.

---

## 1. Stickers

Small weatherproof sticker with lv2park.com URL placed on:
- LV2 sign posts in the zone
- Lamp posts near the zone boundary
- Community bulletin boards (coffee shops, laundromats, gyms near Wrigley)

Design: minimal. Black on yellow (matches site palette). Just the URL and a one-line hook: "Is LV2 active tonight? lv2park.com"

Notes:
- Cheap to produce (Sticker Mule, StickerApp, ~$30-50 for 100)
- High visibility on the actual signs people park near
- Gray area legally on city sign posts -- coffee shops and bulletin boards are safer
- QR code optional -- the URL is short enough to type

---

## 2. Fake Parking Tickets

A printed "warning" that looks like a Chicago parking ticket envelope left on windshields in the LV2 zone on game days. Opens to reveal: "This isn't a real ticket. But next time it might be. lv2park.com -- free daily LV2 alert."

Notes:
- High memorability -- people who get fake-ticketed WILL remember the site
- Needs to be clearly labeled as not a real ticket (don't want someone actually panicking)
- Labor-intensive: someone has to walk the zone and place them on game days
- Best timing: 4:00-4:45 PM on a game day, before enforcement starts at 5
- Could be a one-time guerrilla launch stunt rather than ongoing

---

## Decision when revisiting

Both ideas are offline acquisition plays -- they convert people who are physically in the zone into subscribers. High relevance, low cost. The sticker is passive (set and forget), the fake ticket is active (requires game day presence).

Neither is urgent until the digital GTM is running. Park until digital channels show diminishing returns or Adam wants a physical stunt.
