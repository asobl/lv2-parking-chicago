# Outreach Templates

*Adapt these. Don't copy-paste verbatim. Real outreach sounds like a person, not a press release.*

---

## 1. Reddit -- r/chicago

**Best timing:** Game day morning, or Monday (before the weekly digest topic comes up naturally)
**Goal:** Organic traction, first subscribers, show it's useful not promotional

**Title options:**
- "I FOIAed 9,434 LV2 parking tickets and built a free tool so Wrigleyville residents stop getting towed"
- "Free tool for Lakeview residents: daily LV2 parking alert before Cubs games"
- "Built this after a friend got towed for the third time at Wrigley -- lv2park.com"

**Body:**
```
If you live anywhere near Wrigley, you know the LV2 zone. Cars parked on the wrong street at 5:00 PM on a game day are gone within minutes.

I built lv2park.com to answer one question every day: is LV2 active tonight?

It pulls the Cubs schedule and shows you:
- Is there a game or event today?
- Is LV2 in effect tonight?
- What does the week look like?
- Where can you park safely?

There's also a Monday email digest if you want the week's schedule in your inbox.

The data nerd part: I FOIAed the city and got 5+ years of LV2 ticket data -- 9,434 tickets from 2018-2023. The data analysis is at lv2park.com/blog/lv2-data-analysis.html if you want to see which streets get hit hardest.

Free, no ads (yet), just a useful tool for the neighborhood.
```

**Note:** Don't post the same day as a loss or controversial Cubs moment. Post on a neutral game day or the Monday before a home series.

## 5. Block Club Chicago -- Story Pitch

**Who:** Block Club Chicago reporters covering Lakeview, Wrigleyville, or city data
**Find contact:** blockclubchicago.org -- look for bylines on Lakeview or Cubs coverage, or use their tips form
**Goal:** Story about the FOIA data -- this is the hook, not "I built a website"
**Best timing:** Right before a home series, or after a visible towing incident gets attention

**Subject:** FOIA story: 9,434 LV2 parking tickets reveal where Wrigleyville enforcement hits hardest

```
Hi [reporter name],

I'm reaching out with a data story about LV2 parking enforcement in Lakeview.

I filed a FOIA request with the city and received 5+ years of LV2 ticket data -- 9,434 citations from 2018 to 2023. I analyzed the data and found:

- Which streets have the highest enforcement density (some blocks have 3-4x the average)
- How enforcement patterns have changed year over year
- Game day vs. concert day ticket rates
- The exact window when tows happen (almost all within the first 30 minutes after 5 PM)

I built a free tool for residents at lv2park.com that tells them daily whether LV2 is active, and I published the data analysis at lv2park.com/blog/lv2-data-analysis.html.

This might make a good story for your Lakeview coverage -- it's the first time this data has been publicly analyzed, and it's useful information heading into the summer season.

Happy to share the full dataset, methodology, or get on a call. Let me know if this is something you'd want to cover.

Thanks,
Adam Sobol
lv2park.com
```

## 7. LinkedIn -- Founder Post

**Goal:** Backlink from LinkedIn domain + potential local media/civic connections
**Audience:** Chicago professionals, founders, civic tech people

```
I built something small but useful for my neighborhood.

lv2park.com tells Lakeview residents whether LV2 parking restrictions are active each night near Wrigley Field. One question, one answer, updated daily.

The backstory: A friend got towed three times in two years. Each time was a game she forgot about. The information exists -- the Cubs schedule is public -- but nobody had pulled it into a single "is tonight a tow night?" answer.

The data nerd part: I filed a FOIA request and got 9,434 LV2 ticket records from the city (2018-2023). The data analysis is on the site. Some streets have 3-4x the average ticket rate.

Stack: GitHub Pages, GitHub Actions, Cloudflare Workers, Resend. Total cost: $1/month (domain). Fully automated.

Lesson I keep relearning: the best products answer one question the user is already asking, in the moment they're asking it.
```

---

## Template Notes

- Always lead with the user's problem, not "I built something"
- The FOIA data is always the hook -- 9,434 is a real number that carries weight
- Don't oversell. "Free neighborhood tool" is more honest and more compelling than "platform"
- Have the data analysis URL ready: lv2park.com/blog/lv2-data-analysis.html
- Never say "check it out" at the end. Say what you want: subscribe, share, link, cover the story.
