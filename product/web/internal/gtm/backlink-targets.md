## What Makes a Good Backlink Request

1. **Frame it as a constituent resource, not a link request.** To the alderman: "this is free for your residents." To Block Club: "this is a data story." Never say "can you link to my site."

2. **The FOIA data gives credibility.** You didn't just build a schedule page -- you have unique data from a government records request. That signals effort and legitimacy.

3. **Always link to the data analysis, not just the homepage.** lv2park.com/blog/lv2-data-analysis.html is more link-worthy than the homepage. It has actual content a journalist or editor can reference.

4. **Don't ask for backlinks from sites you have no real relationship with.** A cold "can you link to me?" to a random Chicago blog is noise. Only reach out where there's genuine mutual value.
