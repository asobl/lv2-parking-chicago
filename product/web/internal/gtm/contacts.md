# Contacts -- LV2 Park GTM

*Verify all contacts before reaching out. Names and roles change. This is a starting directory, not a current roster.*

---

## 44th Ward (Lakeview)

The LV2 zone sits almost entirely in the 44th Ward. This is the most direct civic relationship.

| Contact | Role | How to Find | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Current 44th Ward Alderman | Alderman (check for current holder -- Tom Tunney held this seat for years) | chicago.gov/city/en/about/wards/44.html | Best contact is the constituent services email listed on the ward site |
| 44th Ward office | Staff | 44thward.org/contact | Newsletter and social media are run through the office |

**What to ask for:**
- Mention in the ward newsletter (sent to thousands of constituents)
- Link from the ward website's "parking resources" or "Wrigley area" section
- Retweet or share on ward social accounts

**Tone:** Constituent-to-elected-official. "I built this free resource for your residents." Not "can you promote my site."

---

## Block Club Chicago

The most important media target. Block Club covers hyper-local Chicago neighborhoods. Lakeview and Wrigleyville are core beats.

| How to find the right reporter | Method |
|---|---|
| Look at recent Lakeview coverage | blockclubchicago.org -- search "Lakeview" or "Wrigley" -- note the bylines |
| Look at Cubs/parking coverage | Anyone who has covered parking enforcement, towing, or LV2 specifically |
| General tips | blockclubchicago.org/tips or email tips@blockclubchicago.org |

**Story angle:** Data story. "9,434 LV2 tickets FOIAed from the city -- here's what the data shows about where enforcement hits hardest." Not "local resident builds website."

**Timing:** Pitch on a Tuesday or Wednesday before a weekend home series. Avoid pitching on game day mornings (newsroom is busy).

## Notes on Verification

- Alderman: verify current holder before reaching out. Chicago ward elections happen every 4 years.
- Block Club reporters: check current masthead -- Block Club has had turnover. Look at recent bylines.
- Chamber orgs: verify websites are current before emailing. Some neighborhood orgs let their sites go stale.
