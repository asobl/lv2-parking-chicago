Rules

What an LV2 permit actually gets you (and what it doesn't).

The LV2 permit covers one specific situation: parking inside the LV2 zone during Cubs game-day enforcement hours. Every other restriction that applies to that block still applies, permit or no permit.

Published December 3, 2026

An LV2 permit is a narrow instrument. It authorizes one car, on one address's block, during one specific enforcement window. It does not grant immunity from any other parking restriction, including street cleaning, residential permits on other blocks, meters, snow routes, or tow zones.

What the permit does cover

An LV2 permit allows the registered vehicle to park legally inside the LV2 zone during the 5 PM to 10 PM game-day enforcement window. The permit is associated with a specific resident address inside the zone and specific license plate numbers tied to that address.

The coverage is narrow but complete within its scope:

  • The permitted vehicle can park on any legal block within the LV2 boundary
  • During the 5-10 PM enforcement window on any Cubs home game day
  • For the duration of the permit's calendar year validity
  • Including postseason games through the end of the Cubs home schedule

If the block is inside LV2 and the permit is valid and displayed, the car is authorized during the game-day window. No ticket, no tow, no issue on that dimension.

What the permit does NOT cover

Restriction Does LV2 permit apply? What to do instead
Standard residential permit zones on other blocks No Apply for the relevant zone permit separately
Street cleaning No Move the car during the posted cleaning window
Snow routes during active snow events No Move the car when the snow event is declared
Rush hour tow zones on arterials No Do not park on designated tow zone streets during rush hour
Metered parking No Pay the meter, observe the time limit
Loading zones (business hours) No Do not park in loading zones during posted business hours
Fire hydrants and fire lanes No Never park in fire-restricted areas
Temporary event no-parking signs No Move the car before the event restriction begins
Parking outside the LV2 boundary No Check for other permit zones on those blocks

The most common misunderstandings

"My LV2 permit covers the whole neighborhood"

It does not. The permit covers only the LV2 boundary (Irving Park, Addison, Racine, Ashland). Blocks just outside that rectangle, even one street over, are not covered. If the car is outside the LV2 boundary and on a residential permit block, it needs the residential permit for that zone. The LV2 permit is not a substitute.

"I have an LV2 permit so I can park anywhere during the game"

Only inside the LV2 boundary. The permit specifies that scope. Some LV2 permit holders assume the permit acts as a general "game day" pass and park on Clark Street, in loading zones, or in other restricted areas during games. It does not work that way. Those other restrictions apply separately.

"Street cleaning gets suspended during games"

It does not. Street cleaning operates on its own schedule, posted weekly, independent of Cubs home games. Cars on a street during the posted cleaning window get ticketed whether or not there is a game that night. An LV2 permit does not waive street cleaning.

The most common expensive mistake: a resident with an LV2 permit parks on their own block during a street cleaning window because they think the LV2 permit covers it. Ticket is still $65. The permit does not override the cleaning restriction.

"My LV2 permit is good for multiple cars"

One permit covers one vehicle. The permit is registered to a specific license plate. If you have two cars, you need two separate permits. Second and third permits at the same household address can usually be obtained but at incrementally higher cost.

"My permit lets my friends park here during the game"

No. The permit is tied to the vehicle registered to the permit holder. Friends and family need a separate guest pass (for Cubs game days) or they need to park outside the zone. The 47th Ward issues LV2 guest passes for Cubs games; the 44th Ward handles its section differently. See our guest pass guide for how to request one.

When the LV2 permit actually matters

The permit matters specifically during the 5-10 PM Cubs game-day window when the zone is actively enforced. Outside that window, the LV2 permit does not matter because LV2 is not active. A resident without an LV2 permit can legally park on an LV2-only block at 11 AM on a Tuesday (no game) or at 11 PM after a game ends (enforcement over).

For residential blocks that are also covered by standard residential permit zones, the residential permit does the year-round work. The LV2 permit handles the game-day overlay. Most permanent residents of the LV2 zone carry both.

What to do if you only have an LV2 permit and nothing else

If your block is LV2-only (no additional standard residential permit zone), the LV2 permit is all you need for game-day enforcement. Outside of game days, the block is open to any legal parker. Outside of the 5-10 PM window on game days, the block is also open.

If your block is both LV2 and a standard residential permit zone, you need both permits. One is not a substitute for the other.

The Chicago Clerk's office and your ward's aldermanic office can confirm which permits apply to your specific address. Apply for both separately if both are required.

Quick reference

  • LV2 permit covers: Cubs game days, 5-10 PM, inside the LV2 rectangle only
  • LV2 permit does not cover: anything else
  • Street cleaning: separate. Move the car during posted windows.
  • Other permit zones: separate permits for each
  • Meters, tow zones, loading zones, fire lanes: no permit overrides these
  • Guests: need their own guest pass, not your permit

The permit is narrow by design. It solves one specific problem (Cubs game-day enforcement for residents) and does not attempt to be a universal parking pass.
