LV2 parking before 5 PM: what's actually legal and what isn't

The LV2 restriction activates at 5 PM. That does not mean the streets near Wrigley are free-for-all before then. Several other rules still apply, and some of them will get you ticketed well before the game starts.

Published July 30, 2026

The LV2 zone is clear before 5 PM on game days, meaning the residential parking restriction specific to LV2 does not apply. Your car will not be towed for LV2 reasons before 5 PM. But "LV2 is clear" is not the same as "you can park anywhere."

The LV2 zone designation restricts parking on residential streets near Wrigley to permit holders during Cubs home games, starting at 5 PM. Before that window opens, the LV2 rule does not apply. But the streets in and around the zone are still subject to every other Chicago parking ordinance, and on game days those ordinances are actively enforced.

What still applies before 5 PM

Street sweeping restrictions. Many streets near Wrigley have street sweeping schedules that run during the day. Sweeping violations are enforced by a separate crew and are not affected by whether there is a Cubs game that night. If a sweeping window is 8 AM to 10 AM and you park at 7:45 AM, you will get a sweeping ticket regardless of the game schedule. Check the signs on every block before you park.

Meter restrictions. Metered blocks near Wrigley require payment during meter hours, which on most blocks runs to 9 PM regardless of day type. A meter that is not fed is a ticket, game day or not. Meter hours are posted on each meter head.

Time-limited parking zones. Some residential blocks near Wrigley have daytime time limits (1-hour, 2-hour) that are enforced with chalk-and-ticket patrols separate from the LV2 enforcement crew. These apply during posted hours, which typically run from morning through early evening. A 2-hour zone can get you ticketed at 3 PM even if the game is that night.

Fire hydrant, bus stop, loading zone, and crosswalk restrictions. These apply 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. They are enforced on game days and non-game days alike. On busy game days, officers working the neighborhood before 5 PM will write these tickets as they encounter them.

Day games change the calculation

For Cubs day games, the 5 PM activation time is after the game ends. Day games typically start at 1:20 PM and run 3 hours, putting the final out around 4:30 PM. The LV2 zone does not activate until 5 PM regardless of first pitch. This means on a day game, you can legally park in the zone during the game and still need to leave by 5 PM after it ends.

This trips people up. A day game is still an LV2 game. The zone still activates. You just have a different window: arrive after the game if you want to park briefly without a permit, but you must be gone by 5 PM. If the game runs long, that window closes fast.

There is also a practical enforcement note: some officers do begin staging in the zone before 5 PM on game days. They are not writing LV2 tickets before 5, but they are present, and they will write tickets for meter violations, time-limit violations, hydrant violations, and anything else they observe. The presence of officers before 5 PM is real. Only the LV2-specific restriction is not yet in effect.

The clearest answer

Before 5 PM on a Cubs game day: the LV2 restriction does not apply, but all other Chicago parking rules do. Read every sign on the block before you park. Look for sweeping schedules, time limits, meter hours, and no-parking windows. If the block is clear of all restrictions for the time you plan to be there, it is legal to park. You still need to leave before 5 PM if you do not have an LV2 permit.

The safest approach is not to assume the zone is your free parking lot before 5. Treat every block near Wrigley the same way you would treat any residential block in the city: read the signs, understand what applies, and plan your exit before the restriction window opens.
