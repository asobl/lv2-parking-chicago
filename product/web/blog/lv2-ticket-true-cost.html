What an LV2 parking ticket actually costs with late fees and towing

The headline number is $65. The real number depends on what happens next. If your car gets towed and you wait two days to pick it up, you are looking at close to $300. Here is every cost at every stage.

Published October 29, 2026

An LV2 parking ticket starts at $65. But on game days, officers do not just ticket — they also call tows. If your car is towed, you owe the ticket fine plus the tow fee plus daily storage. The longer you wait, the more it costs. Two days after a tow, the total can exceed $280.

These are the Chicago Department of Finance's published rates as of 2026. Tow and storage fees are set by ordinance and are not negotiable. The ticket fine can sometimes be reduced through the adjudication process, but the towing and storage costs are owed to the tow company and impound lot separately, regardless of whether you contest the underlying ticket.

The tow timeline on game days

On Cubs home game days, the LV2 zone activates at 5 PM. Officers are staged and moving immediately. The FOIA ticket data shows enforcement starts within the first minutes after 5 PM, and towing follows closely on the streets with the highest violation density. By 5:15 PM on a busy game night, cars without permits are being hooked on Marshfield and Paulina.

There is no window to quickly move your car once the zone activates. If your car is in the zone at 5:00 PM on a game day without a valid permit, expect a ticket. If officers are working your block, expect a tow. The enforcement machine is running from the first second.

If you discover your car was towed, the Chicago Towed Vehicle Locator at cityofchicago.org can confirm your plate and give you the impound lot address. Most towed vehicles near Wrigley go to city lots. Call the lot before heading there to confirm hours and payment options.

After 25 days, the fine doubles

Chicago parking fines double after 25 days if unpaid. A $65 LV2 ticket becomes $130 on day 26. After 60 days unpaid, the city can refer the debt to collections, add administrative fees, and ultimately boot or tow your vehicle for the outstanding balance even when there is no current violation.

Multiple unpaid tickets compound fast. Two $65 tickets left unpaid for 30 days become $260. Three become $390. The city also reports unpaid tickets to the Illinois Secretary of State, which can block your ability to renew your license plate registration.

Contesting the ticket

You can contest an LV2 parking ticket online at cityofchicago.org/finance, by mail, or in person at the Department of Administrative Hearings. Common defenses: the car had a valid LV2 permit that was not recorded correctly, the car was not at the cited location at the cited time, or there was a signage error on the specific block. Contesting does not suspend the 25-day clock for fee doubling while the hearing is pending. If you win, the original fine is waived. If you lose, you still owe the original amount plus any late fees that have accumulated.

The tow fee and storage are separate from the ticket adjudication. Winning the ticket hearing does not get you a refund on the tow. Those costs are owed to the tow company regardless of the ticket outcome.
