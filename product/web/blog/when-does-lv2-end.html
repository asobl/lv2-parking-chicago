When does LV2 parking end for the season?

LV2 is not tied to a calendar date. It activates on Cubs home game days and stays dormant between them. When the Cubs play their last home game of the year, LV2 goes dormant until next April.

Published November 5, 2026

The last Cubs home game ends LV2 for the year. If the team misses the postseason, that's the last regular season home game, usually the final weekend of September. If the team makes the playoffs, LV2 extends through the last playoff game at Wrigley. Either way, the zone goes dormant once the home schedule is exhausted.

The mechanical rule

LV2 activates at 5 PM on any day a Cubs home game is played at Wrigley Field. If there is no Cubs home game scheduled, LV2 does not activate. This is true for a random Tuesday in February and a Saturday in late October after the Cubs have been eliminated.

The zone does not formally "close" for the season. There is no city announcement, no signs coming down, no ordinance change. The zone is always on the books. It just sits dormant because the trigger (a home game) is no longer happening.

In practice, LV2 signs remain posted year-round. They become informational rather than active. Residents keep their permits on their cars through the winter because some still keep the permits affixed year-round as a habit, and because renewal cycles for LV2 permits sync to the full calendar year, not the baseball season.

The three scenarios for "when it ends"

  • Cubs miss the postseason (most common): LV2 ends after the last regular season home game, typically the final weekend of September. In a 162-game MLB schedule, the Cubs usually have their last home game between September 24 and October 1.
  • Cubs make the playoffs but don't host: Same as above. If the Cubs qualify for the Wild Card but play their Wild Card game on the road, or are eliminated before getting a home game, LV2 ends with the last regular season home game.
  • Cubs make the playoffs and host games: LV2 extends through the last home playoff game. In the deepest possible run (making the World Series and having Game 7 at Wrigley), LV2 would extend into early November.

The specific dates for the 2026 season

The 2026 Cubs home schedule ends the weekend of September 26-28 based on the MLB regular season calendar. If the Cubs miss the postseason, LV2 enforcement ends after Sunday September 28, 2026. The zone does not activate for the rest of the calendar year.

If the Cubs make the postseason:

  • Wild Card round home games: late September or early October
  • NLDS home games (Games 1, 2, potential Game 5): first week of October
  • NLCS home games (Games 3, 4, potential Game 5): second week of October
  • World Series home games (Games 3, 4, potential Game 5): late October

A deep run could push the last LV2 day into the first week of November. A shallow run ends LV2 within a few days of the regular season.

The LV2 Park homepage shows the next scheduled Cubs home game and whether LV2 is active today. When the homepage shows no upcoming home games, the season is effectively over.

What stays restricted after LV2 ends

LV2 going dormant does not mean the Wrigley area becomes free parking. The other restrictions still apply:

  • Residential permit zones are year-round. If a block is in a permit zone, it is still permit-only after LV2 ends.
  • Street cleaning runs through November 30. Signs posted weekly. Enforcement continues until the end of the cleaning season.
  • Meter zones do not change. Meters keep their posted hours year-round.
  • Loading zones, bus stops, fire lanes are permanent. These do not vary by season.
  • Snow routes activate when snow events occur. Cars parked on posted snow routes during active events get towed regardless of the time of year.
The biggest mistake after LV2 ends: assuming the neighborhood is open parking. Residential permit zones stay enforced. A car parked on a permit block in November still gets ticketed even though the LV2 window is dormant. The zone rules and the permit zones are two separate things.

Off-season events at Wrigley

Wrigley hosts non-Cubs events in the off-season: college football games, winter ice skating, concerts, and miscellaneous corporate events. These events do NOT trigger LV2. The ordinance language specifically ties the zone to Cubs home games, not to any event at Wrigley Field.

Non-Cubs events do sometimes trigger other restrictions (temporary no-parking signs posted 72 hours in advance, pedestrian perimeter closures, rideshare pickup zones). These are event-specific and do not require a Cubs permit. Read the signs for each event.

When LV2 comes back

LV2 re-activates when the Cubs play their first home game of the following season, usually the first week of April. The MLB schedule is released in the late summer or early fall of the prior year, so the exact first home game date is known months in advance.

Spring training games and exhibition games at Wrigley (rare) do not trigger LV2. The ordinance specifies regular season and postseason games, not exhibitions.

Quick reference

  • LV2 dormant: day after last Cubs home game (regular season or postseason) through first Cubs home game the following season
  • LV2 active: 5 PM to 10 PM on any day a Cubs home game is played
  • Other restrictions: unchanged year-round
