The best neighborhoods to park near Wrigley and walk in.

The smartest move on a Cubs game day is to park two neighborhoods away, walk 15 to 25 minutes, and skip the LV2 zone entirely. Here's where that actually works.

Published October 8, 2026

The best park-and-walk neighborhoods share three traits: minimal permit zone coverage, enough residential streets with open parking, and a safe walk to Wrigley in under 25 minutes. Five neighborhoods check all three boxes. Most of the others do not.

The rules of smart park-and-walk

  • Get out of the LV2 zone. The boundaries are Irving Park (north), Addison (south), Racine (east), Ashland (west).
  • Avoid other residential permit zones. Many surrounding blocks have 24/7 permit requirements that predate LV2.
  • Check for street cleaning. April through November, every block has a posted cleaning window. Read the signs before you leave the car for 4 hours.
  • Favor streets with business activity or main-street frontage. These are less likely to be permit-restricted and feel safer at night.
Option 1 · 20-min walk · Best for day games

Roscoe Village

Directly west of the LV2 zone, starting around Damen Avenue. The blocks between Roscoe, Belmont, and Addison west of Damen have a mix of permit and non-permit streets with reasonable street parking availability. The walk east to Wrigley is flat, well-lit, and takes 18 to 22 minutes depending on your starting block.

Good for: day games, pairs or small groups, anyone who wants a coffee or lunch before the game. Belmont and Roscoe have block after block of restaurants.

Watch out for: street cleaning on Tuesdays and Wednesdays April through November. Some blocks have residential permit requirements even though they are outside LV2.

Option 2 · 15-20 min walk · Best for post-game exit

Lakeview East (east of Racine)

The blocks east of Racine Avenue between Belmont and Irving Park are outside the LV2 boundary but close enough to Wrigley to walk in 15 minutes. Many blocks have metered parking that becomes free after 10 PM or on Sundays. Non-metered residential blocks exist but mix with permit zones.

Good for: night games, anyone who wants a short walk, fans coming from the east side of the city via Lake Shore Drive.

Watch out for: meter hours run late (often until 10 PM). Some blocks directly adjacent to Broadway have commercial loading zones and bus stops that confuse newcomers.

Option 3 · 25-30 min walk · Best for full-day visits

Andersonville

North of LV2 starting around Foster Avenue. Clark Street runs through Andersonville and continues south directly to Wrigley, which makes the walk easy and well-lit. Most residential side streets have minimal permit coverage. The trade-off is distance: a 25 to 30 minute walk is not trivial in cold weather or after a long game.

Good for: day games, fans who want to make dinner or drinks in Andersonville part of the trip, groups who do not want to deal with post-game traffic.

Watch out for: the return walk in cold weather. An alternative is to take the Clark Street bus (CTA #22) back to your car.

Option 4 · 30+ min walk · Best if you're OK with Divvy

North Center and Lincoln Square

West and northwest of the LV2 zone, roughly between Western Avenue and Damen north of Addison. Permit coverage is lighter here than in Lakeview. Residential streets off Lincoln Avenue and Irving Park Road have meaningful availability even on game nights. The walk is long (30 to 40 minutes) but Divvy bike stations are plentiful and turn the trip into a 10 to 12 minute ride.

Good for: fans with mobility to bike, game-day cyclists, anyone coming from the northwest suburbs who wants to avoid Lake Shore Drive entirely.

Watch out for: the return bike ride at 10 PM in poor weather. A ride back to the car via CTA bus or the Brown Line (to Addison or Western) is the backup plan.

Option 5 · 20-min walk · Best if you know the area

Southport Corridor (south of Addison)

The blocks between Southport Avenue and Ashland, south of Addison down to Belmont, are technically outside LV2. Permit coverage here is more extensive than the other options, so free parking is harder to find, but a patient driver can usually find a legal spot within 10 to 15 minutes of searching. The walk from Southport and Belmont to Wrigley is 15 to 20 minutes up Southport then east on Addison.

Good for: locals who know which blocks are permit-free, anyone who enjoys the Southport restaurant scene before a game.

Watch out for: heavy permit zone coverage means reading every sign on every block. Park in the wrong spot and you get the same $65 ticket you would have gotten inside LV2.

Neighborhoods to avoid

  • Anywhere inside LV2 without a permit. Obvious but worth repeating. The enforcement machine starts at 5 PM sharp.
  • Wrigleyville proper, north of Addison and south of Irving Park between Sheffield and Racine. This is the core LV2 enforcement zone. Ticket and tow pressure is highest here.
  • Directly on Clark Street between Addison and Waveland. Mix of loading zones, pedestrian-only during some games, and heavy security perimeter.
  • Anywhere you see signs you cannot read quickly. If the sign has four lines and you cannot parse them in 15 seconds, keep driving.
A 20-minute walk saves $65 in tickets, $175 to $310 in tows, and 30 to 60 minutes in post-game traffic. It also adds 40 minutes of exercise to your day. For most fans, this is the single best move available on a Cubs game day.

What to bring for a long walk

  • A jacket for the walk back. October night games get cold even if the afternoon was warm.
  • Good shoes. A 20-minute walk is different from the 5-minute walk from your garage reservation.
  • Phone battery. GPS navigation back to an unfamiliar street helps on the return trip.
  • A photo of where you parked. Sounds obvious. People still forget. Take a picture of the street sign and the closest storefront before you walk away.

The transit alternative

Any of these neighborhoods can also be a transit starting point instead of a parking solution. Park further out, take the CTA the rest of the way. Brown Line stops at Addison, Paulina, Southport, Belmont and connects to the Red Line at Fullerton. Bus routes #22 (Clark) and #151 (Sheridan) run directly to Wrigley from north and south respectively.
