LV2 during the Cubs postseason: exactly what the rules say.

The 2023 amendment to Chicago's LV2 ordinance made one thing explicit that the previous version left ambiguous. Postseason games are home games. The restrictions apply. Here is the precise rule and the edge cases.

Published October 1, 2026

LV2 applies to every Cubs home game scheduled at Wrigley Field, including Wild Card, Division Series, Championship Series, and World Series games. Enforcement runs 5 PM to 10 PM on any day a game is played or scheduled to be played. The rule does not distinguish between regular season and postseason.

The 2023 ordinance change

Prior to 2023, LV2 enforcement was tied to a list of "night games" and specific events published in advance by the city. The pre-2023 ordinance language made night games the default trigger and treated day games as a separate category. Postseason games fell into a gray zone that some residents argued should be exempt.

The 2023 amendment simplified this. The current language in Chicago Municipal Code 9-68-023 ties LV2 enforcement to any day a Cubs home game is scheduled at Wrigley Field, regardless of time of day, day of week, or whether the game is regular season or postseason. The practical effect: if there is a Cubs home game on the calendar for that day, LV2 activates at 5 PM.

What the ordinance says

The controlling language: parking in the LV2 zone during enforcement hours is restricted to holders of valid LV2 permits or authorized guest passes. The enforcement window is 5:00 PM to 10:00 PM on any day a Cubs home game is played at Wrigley Field.

"Played at Wrigley Field" is the operative phrase. It covers regular season home games, any postseason series where the Cubs host games, any tiebreaker or play-in games at Wrigley, and any makeup games rescheduled to Wrigley. It does not cover games where the Cubs are the away team, even if that game is in the postseason.

Every postseason scenario the rule covers

  • Wild Card game at Wrigley: LV2 applies. Standard 5-10 PM enforcement.
  • NLDS home games (Games 1, 2, and 5): LV2 applies on each home game day.
  • NLCS home games (Games 3, 4, 5, if needed): LV2 applies on each home game day.
  • World Series home games (Games 3, 4, 5, if needed): LV2 applies on each home game day.
  • Tiebreaker game at Wrigley: LV2 applies. This is treated as a home game.
  • Play-in game at Wrigley: LV2 applies if the Cubs are the home team.
  • Cubs are visitors in a postseason game: LV2 does NOT apply. No home game at Wrigley.

Rain-outs and postponements

If a postseason game is rained out or postponed, LV2 enforcement for that day depends on what happens with the scheduled game. If the game is postponed to a later date, LV2 still activates at 5 PM on the originally scheduled day if enforcement preparations are already in place, but the city typically pulls enforcement once the postponement is announced if the announcement comes before 5 PM.

If the announcement comes after 5 PM (for example, a first-pitch rain-out called at 7 PM), LV2 enforcement continues for that evening. Cars without permits that were parked after 5 PM are still subject to tickets even if the game never happens.

For the rescheduled date, LV2 activates normally at 5 PM on the new game day. Check the LV2 Park homepage each day to see the current status.

Extra innings past 10 PM

The ordinance specifies the enforcement window as 5 PM to 10 PM. If a Cubs postseason game goes into extra innings and runs past 10 PM, LV2 enforcement formally ends at 10 PM regardless of whether the game is still in progress.

In practice, officers are already finishing their shifts at 10 PM and ticket volume drops sharply in the final minutes. By 10:05 PM, active enforcement has usually stopped. The zone technically reopens to non-permit parking at 10:00 PM even if 30,000 people are still in the ballpark waiting for a walk-off.

This creates a narrow window right at 10 PM where cars can legally park in the zone while the game is still being played. If you parked outside the zone pre-game and want to move closer to your exit, waiting until 10:00 PM is the legal move.

Series that end early

Postseason series end when one team reaches the required win count. If the Cubs sweep an NLDS in three games (two home, one away), the scheduled Games 4 and 5 at Wrigley never happen. LV2 does not activate on those dates.

Conversely, if the Cubs are eliminated on the road before a planned home game, LV2 for that home game is cancelled. The zone does not activate for a scheduled game that becomes unnecessary due to series elimination.

The rule is straightforward: if a Cubs home game is actually being played at Wrigley, LV2 applies. If the game is cancelled, postponed before 5 PM, or eliminated by series outcome, LV2 does not apply for that date.

Makeup games

Makeup games that are rescheduled to Wrigley trigger LV2 on the new date. This includes both regular season games rescheduled to postseason dates (rare but possible) and postseason games rescheduled for weather.

Double-headers (two games on the same day) trigger a single LV2 enforcement window, not two. The 5-10 PM window covers both games on the same calendar day.

Permit validity during postseason

LV2 permits issued for the regular season are valid through the postseason. Permit expiration dates are set to October 31 or later for each season, specifically to cover any potential playoff run. If the Cubs make a deep postseason run that stretches into early November, standard permits remain valid through the last home game.

Guest passes work the same way. A guest pass issued in August for a specific game date can only be used for that date. For a postseason home game, a new guest pass for that specific date is required.

See our guide to Cubs game guest passes for the application process.

Fines are the same

The penalty structure does not change for postseason games. A ticket is $65, doubles after 25 days unpaid, and can be contested through the standard Chicago adjudication process. If the car is towed, the tow fee is $175 plus $35 per day of storage.

The only thing different in the postseason is the likelihood of getting a ticket. Playoff crowds bring more unfamiliar drivers who park in the zone without knowing about LV2. Enforcement intensity is consistent, but the available targets are denser.
