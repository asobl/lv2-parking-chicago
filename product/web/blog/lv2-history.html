History
Published June 18, 2026

The history of LV2 parking near Wrigley Field.

LV2 started as a night-game-only program to protect Lakeview and Uptown residents from game-day parking chaos. It's changed significantly since. Here's the full timeline.

The most important change happened in 2023: LV2 expanded from night games only to all Cubs home games, including day games and postseason. If you haven't updated your habits since 2022, this is the change that matters.

Why LV2 exists

Before Wrigley Field had lights, daytime games drew manageable crowds and parking was a minor inconvenience. Lights were installed in 1988, and night games changed everything for the surrounding neighborhood. A 7 PM game means thousands of cars arriving between 4 and 6 PM, filling every residential street within half a mile.

Residents in Lakeview and Uptown — many of whom had lived in the neighborhood long before night games existed — pushed the city for a system that would protect their ability to park near their own homes. The result was a dedicated permit program, operating under Chicago Municipal Code Section 9-68-023.

Key milestones

  • 1988
    Wrigley Field gets lights
    The first night game at Wrigley is played August 8, 1988. Night games become regular in 1989. Residential parking congestion in Lakeview escalates quickly. Residents begin pressing the city and aldermen for relief.
  • Early 2000s
    LV2 permit program established
    The City of Chicago creates the Wrigley Field Resident Permit Parking Program under the LV2 zone designation. Enforcement applies on night game dates, 5 PM to 10 PM. Permits are issued by aldermanic offices in the 44th, 46th, and 47th Wards covering the zone.
  • 2009
    Formal program documentation published
    The City of Chicago publishes the 2009 Wrigley Field Resident Permit Parking Program brochure. At this point the program covers night games and concerts only. Permit eligibility requires a current Chicago City Vehicle Sticker, license plate number, and proof of residency. Permits are free and mailed before the first night game. Towing to Auto Pound #6 at 701 N. Sacramento; minimum retrieval cost $160. Four wards manage permits: 32nd (Alderman Waguespack), 44th (Alderman Tunney), 46th (Alderman Shiller), 47th (Alderman Schulter). Violations hotline: 866-4-CPD-TOW.
  • 2010
    Zone 383 formalized as a separate zone
    A 2010 advisory clarifies that Zone 383 — covering streets bounded by Irving Park Road, Broadway, Addison Street, and Ashland Avenue — is now its own residential permit zone. Zone 383 residents do not receive LV2 permits. LV2 covers streets north of Irving Park, west of Ashland, and east of Broadway. The two zones are not interchangeable: LV2 permits don't work in Zone 383 and vice versa. Annual Zone 383 permits cost $25 plus the vehicle sticker. Single-day guest permits become available at $8 per book of 15.
  • 2010s
    Program continues with night games and concerts
    LV2 operates consistently as a night-game and concert program through the 2010s. Residents receive permits by mail before each season. Aldermanic offices change hands over the years as ward representatives are elected and re-elected, but the program structure stays the same. Day games remain outside LV2 enforcement scope.
  • 2023 — Major Change
    LV2 expands to ALL Cubs home games
    The most significant change in the program's history: LV2 now applies to every Cubs home game, including day games and postseason games, not just night games. The change was communicated to residents via email from aldermanic offices before the 2023 season. The reason, as explained by residents who received the email: too many fans were attending day games, drinking, and leaving their cars in the neighborhood overnight. The 5 PM–10 PM enforcement window remains the same, but it now activates on afternoon game days too — even when the game started at 1:20 PM and the stadium empties by 4 PM.
  • 2026
    Current program
    LV2 restrictions apply from 5 PM to 10 PM on all Cubs home game dates (including postseason) and all concerts at Wrigley Field. Permits are managed by the 44th, 46th, and 47th Wards. The 47th Ward (Alderman Mat Martin) offers an online permit application with mail delivery. Enforcement is immediate at 5 PM on covered days. This site tracks the daily status using the MLB schedule and Ticketmaster concert data.

What hasn't changed

Despite the 2023 expansion, several things remain exactly as they were in 2009:

  • Enforcement hours: 5 PM to 10 PM
  • Towing destination: Auto Pound #6, 701 N. Sacramento
  • Violations hotline: 866-4-CPD-TOW (866-427-3869)
  • Permit requirement: current City Vehicle Sticker + proof of residency
  • Permits may not be transferred, purchased, or sold (Municipal Code 9-68-020)
  • LV2 and Zone 383 are separate and not interchangeable
