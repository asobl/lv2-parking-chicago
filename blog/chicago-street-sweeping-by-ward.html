Chicago street sweeping schedules near Wrigley: what the signs mean and when to move your car.

Sweeping season runs April through November. Near Wrigley, this overlaps with Cubs season — which means some days you're moving your car for both LV2 and street sweeping.

A street sweeping ticket in Chicago is typically $60–$100. It's not dramatic, but it's also completely avoidable. The schedule is public, the signs are on every block, and every ward publishes its calendar.

How Chicago street sweeping works

The Chicago Department of Streets and Sanitation runs mechanical sweepers through residential streets to clean debris, leaves, and standing water. Sweeping happens on a fixed schedule — usually one or two days per month per side of the street — during the season from approximately April 1 through November 30.

Parking enforcement officers precede the sweeper and ticket any car that hasn't moved. The sweeper itself doesn't tow, but if you're still there after the officers pass, you'll have a ticket waiting. In some cases, if you're blocking the sweeper path, a tow is possible.

Reading the street sweeping sign

Typical sign text: "Street Cleaning / 9 AM–3 PM / 3rd Monday of Month / Apr 1–Nov 30 / Weather Permitting / Tow Zone"

  • 9 AM–3 PM: The window when you can't park. Move your car before 9 AM and don't return until after 3 PM.
  • 3rd Monday of Month: This applies only once a month. Mark your calendar — don't assume it's every Monday.
  • Apr 1–Nov 30: The season. Outside these dates, the restriction doesn't apply even if the sign is still there.
  • Weather Permitting: The city cancels sweeping if it rained heavily. Check CDSS announcements or 311 on uncertain days. The restriction still technically applies unless an official cancellation is posted.
  • Tow Zone: The city can tow, not just ticket. Usually enforcement is a ticket, but the tow authority exists.

Wards near Wrigley Field: schedule resources

Street sweeping schedules are set at the ward level and published by each alderman's office. Here are the four wards that cover the LV2 / Zone 383 area:

44th Ward
Covers Lakeview / Wrigleyville — the core Wrigley neighborhood. Publishes annual street sweeping calendars, typically available at the start of sweeping season.
44thward.org
46th Ward
Covers Uptown and parts of Lakeview north of Wrigley. Street sweeping info and parking resources available at the alderman's site.
46thward.com/parking
47th Ward (Alderman Mat Martin)
Covers the North Center / Ravenswood edge of the LV2 zone. The 47th Ward is the most digitally active of the three — alderman Martin's office publishes ward updates and resources online. Check the parking section for sweeping schedules.
aldermanmartin.com/parking

The double problem: LV2 days that are also sweeping days

Near Wrigley, a Cubs day game and a street sweeping day can fall on the same date. That means your car needs to be off the street for sweeping hours (e.g., 9 AM–3 PM), and it also needs to be off by 5 PM for LV2.

If there's a 1:20 PM day game and your street sweeps on the first Tuesday of the month: you need to move your car before 9 AM and you can't bring it back to an LV2 street after the game ends (around 4:30–5 PM) without a permit.

The safest approach on double-restriction days: park outside the LV2 zone entirely, in an area that doesn't sweep on that day.

How to find the citywide schedule

The City of Chicago also maintains a street sweeping lookup tool where you can enter your address and see the sweeping schedule for your specific block:

  • Go to chicago.gov street sweeping lookup
  • Enter your street address
  • The tool returns the day-of-month and time for both sides of your block

You can also set up street sweeping reminders through the city's 311 service or through third-party apps that integrate with city schedule data.
