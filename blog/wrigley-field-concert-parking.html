Wrigley Field concert parking: how it differs from a Cubs game.

LV2 still activates for concerts. But the schedule, lots, and transit situation change. Here's what to plan for.

LV2 restrictions apply from 5 PM to 10 PM on all concert dates at Wrigley Field — the same window as Cubs games. If you're a resident or visitor, the enforcement rules are identical. The parking experience, however, is different.

How concerts are different from game days

A few things shift when Wrigley hosts a concert instead of a Cubs game:

  • The crowd is different. Concert-goers are often coming from across the region, not just north-side regulars. More people looking for street parking who don't know LV2 exists.
  • No Cubs shuttle. The free Cubs remote lot shuttle runs for baseball games only. For concerts, you're on your own for transportation.
  • Paid lots behave differently. SpotHero and garage operators often price concert nights higher than mid-week games. Book in advance.
  • Concert timing varies. Most shows start at 7 PM or later. The 5 PM enforcement window hits while people are still arriving, which means more confusion and more tows.

LV2 enforcement is the same

There's no version of LV2 that's more relaxed for concerts. The City treats concerts at Wrigley the same as game days for enforcement purposes. Officers are staged and ready at 5 PM. If your car is on an LV2 street without a valid permit at 5:00 PM, it gets towed.

This has been true since the program began. The original 2009 program documentation stated explicitly: "The parking permit is also valid for concerts that take place at Wrigley Field." Both directions apply — the restriction is also in effect for concerts.

How to check if a Wrigley concert triggers LV2

The LV2 Park homepage shows today's and this week's event schedule, including concerts. If a concert is at Wrigley, LV2 will be marked as active for that date.

For the full Cubs concert schedule, check mlb.com/cubs/tickets/concerts — the Cubs post their Wrigley concert lineup there. Ticketmaster also lists all Wrigley events.

Parking options for concerts

Option Notes
SpotHero lots Reserve in advance — prices rise as the show approaches. Multiple lots within 5 minutes walk.
Street parking outside the zone North of Irving Park Rd or south of Belmont Ave is outside the LV2 zone, though often still crowded on concert nights.
Red Line (Addison stop) Easiest option if you're coming from the south. Runs until after midnight.
Uber/Lyft drop-off Drop-off and pickup on Clark St or Addison St. Surge pricing after shows ends.
Divvy / scooters Good for shorter distances. See our Divvy guide.

For residents: same rules, same process

If you live in the LV2 zone, your annual resident permit covers concert nights exactly the same as game nights. You don't need to do anything extra. If you have visitors coming for a concert, your LV2 game-day guest passes cover concert dates too — the passes aren't baseball-specific.
